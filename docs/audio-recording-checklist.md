# 🎙️ Audio Recording Checklist — the real teaching voice

**Why this is the most important content task.** Level 1 is *audio-first, no
letters* — the child learns by **hearing** sounds. Right now every clip is browser
text-to-speech (TTS), which **mispronounces isolated phonemes** (it says letter
*names*, not sounds) and is **not Barton-correct**. Recorded human voice is the
accurate teaching signal. This is the gap that most affects real learning.

**Good news — zero risk to ship.** The app already plays recorded clips when they
exist and falls back to TTS when they don't. So you can record in any order, drop
files in, and each one upgrades that sound/word instantly. Nothing breaks while the
list is incomplete.

---

## How to record (simple)
- Any phone voice-memo app or laptop mic in a **quiet room** is fine.
- One clip per sound/word. Keep it **short** and natural; leave a tiny bit of
  silence trimmed off the front.
- Export as **`.mp3`** (mono is fine, small files).
- Name each file **exactly** as listed below (lowercase), then drop it in the
  matching folder:
  - phonemes → `public/audio/sounds/`
  - words → `public/audio/words/`
- Tell me when a batch is in and I'll verify them live.

> **Pronunciation rule (critical):** say the **pure sound**, not the letter name.
> `m` = "mmm" (not "em"), `t` = "t" clipped (not "tee"), `s` = "sss", `a` = short
> "a" as in *cat* (not "ay"). Stop sounds (t, p, b, k, d, g) should be crisp with
> no "uh" tail ("t", not "tuh").
