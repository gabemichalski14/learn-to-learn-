## Universal architecture (holds across all books)

Every level's book follows the same shape (confirmed L2–L7):

**Overview → Lessons → (Games) → Tips & Error Correction → Optional Posttest →
Student Pages.**

- **Overview / "Procedures in Each Lesson"** — the repeating lesson routine + a
  one-page summary of the level's big ideas + the pronunciation key.
- **Lessons** — each lesson = Review → New Teaching (a sound / rule / unit) →
  Read Words → Spell Words → Read & Spell Nonsense Words on Tiles → Read Phrases
  → Read & Mark Sentences → Read a Story. (Exact labels vary slightly per level.)
- **Tips & Error Correction** — targeted correction routines (inform our gentle
  retry hints; summarized, never copied).
- **Optional Posttest** — a no-fail mastery check on the level's skills →
  maps onto our tutor-dashboard "level check."

### Pronunciation key / notation (from the Overview pages)

Barton marks vowels with breve/macron and gives a keyword anchor:

| Symbol | Sound | (we anchor with our own common keyword) |
|--------|-------|------------------------------------------|
| `/ă/` | short a | cat / apple |
| `/ĕ/` | short e | egg / Ed |
| `/ĭ/` | short i | itch / igloo |
| `/ŏ/` | short o | ox / olive |
| `/ŭ/` | short u | up / umbrella |
| `/ā/` | long a | acorn / apron |
| `/ē/` | long e | eat / teeth |
| `/ī/` | long i | ice / idea |
| `/ō/` | long o | open |
| `/ū/`, `/o͞o/` | the two sounds of long u | few · true |

Consonant digraphs/units use lowercase letter clusters (sh, th, ch, wh, ck, …);
schwa is `/ə/`; vowel-r is written as the letter pair (ar, or, er, ir, ur).

**Sound Chart — articulatory facts** (frame l1d/f034 right; linguistic fact, not
Barton expression):
- Vowel anchors A/E/I/O/U (we use our own keywords).
- **Pure sounds:** clip the /uh/ off every consonant — never let the chin drop
  after the sound. (Drives our audio: record clean, unvoiced-schwa phonemes.)
- **Fast sounds** (hardest to hear): b c d g h j k p qu t w y ch → if struggling,
  use them only at word-end first, then move to word-start.
- **Quiet (voiceless) sounds:** c f h k p s t x th sh.
- **Vibrating (voiced):** all vowels (slow) + voiced consonants.
- **Hardest = fast + voiced:** b d g j w y.
- **Excluded from PA training:** k, qu, x (starred) — redundant with c / are
  2-sound blends.

**Game mapping:** Break Apart → *Tap It Out* (segment) · Break/Replace/Remove →
*Switch It* (manipulate) · Compare Two Words → *Same or Different?* (compare).

## Level 4 — Syllable Division & Vowel Teams ✅ (verified + retitled)
_Book 4. © 2000 Susan Barton, v1.7. **Running header on every interior page:**
"Book #4: Syllable Division & Vowel Teams" — the TOC's centered title used the
short form "Syllable Division" (which is why it was briefly trimmed; reverted to
the fuller running-header title). 14 lessons verified from the contents-page
still._

**Lesson 1 "Open Syllables" — Materials & Overview** (frame l4r/f002):
- New tiles: **Vowel Y (yellow)** + a 2nd full A–Z set. Letter tiles A–Z plus
  digraphs CH TH CK SH WH TCH. **Unit tiles:** ALL, ANG, ING, ONG, UNG, ANK,
  INK, ONK, UNK, OLL, OLD, OLT, OST, ILD, IND (the glued units from Book 3).
- **Six syllable types framing:** by Book 4 the student knows **Closed** + **Unit**;
  this book adds **Open** (then later Vowel-Team etc.). Sprinkle the two diagnostic
  questions every lesson: *"What kind of syllable — Open, Closed, or Unit?"* and
  *"What sound is the vowel making — Long or Short?"*
- **Open syllable** = one vowel at the end → says its **name** (long sound).
- **Long-U → two sounds:** Few `/ē-oo/` vs Flu `/oo/`.
- **Vowel-Y:** long **I** at end of 1-syllable words (sky); long **E** at end of
  longer words; American words don't end in I, so spell final long-I with Y.
- **No finger-spelling** is used anywhere in Book 4.

**Per-lesson routine** (Progress Tracking Sheet, l4r/f002 left): Review →
new teaching → read words → spell words → **sight-word review (reading deck +
spelling cards)** → spell sentences → read a story.

**Correction made:** title reverted to "Syllable Division & Vowel Teams" (running
header). Lesson list (14) already verified from the contents still — unchanged.

## Level 7 — Vowel-R Syllables ✅ (verified + enriched)
_Book 7. © 2001 Susan Barton, v1.1._

11 lessons verified from the contents page (single page, ends at Optional
Posttest p429). **Overview page** (frame l7r/f006):
- **Vowel-R** = a new syllable type: a vowel followed by R makes a unique sound
  (neither long nor short). R is a "Rotten Letter"; the vowel **and** the R sit on
  the **same yellow tile**.
- **AR & OR**: distinctive when **accented**; switch to `/er/` when **unaccented**
  (dollar, janitor). **ER, IR, UR** all say `/er/`.
- **EAR** has three sounds — Hear / Earl / Swear; when decoding an unknown word try
  them in the order `/eer/ → /er/ → /air/`.
- `/air/` has many spellings (pear, pair, parent…).
- Spelling strategy: for an ambiguous `/er/`, use a spell-checker, entering a "?"
  for the vowel (SH?RT). (Real-world tool use baked into the method.)
- **Vowel-R key:** ar=car · er=her · ir=bird · or=corn · ur=burp · ear=hear/earl/
  swear. **No corrections.**
