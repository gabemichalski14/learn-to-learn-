## What it is
A **pre-reading program** that builds the underlying **auditory-processing** skills
that are commonly weak in dyslexia — *before* letters/reading. It is a **readiness
/ precursor to the Barton Reading & Spelling System** (Barton's "Brick C"
readiness). No prior teaching experience needed; online training; physical kit or
virtual option.

## Who it's for
Pre-readers and struggling readers / students with dyslexia who aren't yet ready
for Level 1, flagged by Susan Barton's student screening (the auditory-skills part).

## The "4 Bricks of the Reading Foundation" (skill areas)
1. **HEAR** — Auditory **Discrimination**: detect and tell apart similar sounds.
2. **RETAIN** — Auditory **Memory**: hold and recall sounds.
3. **SEQUENCE** — Auditory **Sequencing**: recall sounds in the correct order.
4. **UNDERSTAND** — **Phonemic Awareness**: words are made of individual sounds.

## Structure (from the handbook + site)
- **12 scripted lessons.** Each lesson centers on a **confusable sound pair**, then
  reviews cumulatively with mixed sound sequences.
  - Confirmed pairs/sounds: L1 **/m/ & /n/** · L2 **/f/ & /v/** · L5 **/v/ & /th/** ·
    L7 introduces **/u/** · L8 **/a/ & /o/**. (Early lessons = confusable consonant
    pairs; later lessons add the short vowels. Full per-lesson list is on the
    handbook's Error-Tracking pages — partially captured; a clean photo of those
    pages would complete the exact sequence.)
- **Per-lesson procedures** (from Error-Tracking headers):
  - Teach / discriminate the new sound(s) — "Making Sounds" articulation.
  - **Procedure 2 — Sequencing with Sound Pictures**: hear a sequence (e.g.
    `M‑N‑M`, `K‑K‑I`) and reproduce it with picture cards.
  - **Procedure 3 — Sequencing with Colored Tiles**: same, with blank colored
    tiles (no letters) — pure sound order.
  - **Error Tracking** + **Progress Tracking Sheet** per lesson (records
    student response / correct / repeat / wrong — like our session+mastery logs).
- Handbook contents: Making Sounds Key (p1) · Progress Tracking Sheet (p3) ·
  Notes (p4) · Error Tracking (p7).

## "Making Sounds Key" — articulatory classification (linguistic fact)
Each sound is taught with a keyword + how the mouth makes it + two factual tags:
**Lip vs Tongue** sound, and **Vibrating (voiced) vs Quiet (voiceless)**. (We use
our own keywords; the classification itself is phonetics.)
- **Lip sounds:** m, f, v, s, sh, b, p (made at the lips/teeth-lip).
- **Tongue sounds:** n, th, k, g, l, r, d, t, ch, j.
- **Vibrating (voiced):** m, n, v, g, r, d, j, b + all vowels.
- **Quiet (voiceless):** f, th, k, s, sh, t, ch, p.
- Confusable pairs are typically same place/manner differing only in voicing
  (f/v, p/b, t/d, k/g, ch/j) or same manner/place family (m/n, s/sh, v/th) — which
  is exactly why FIS drills them as pairs.
- Sound inventory taught: consonants **m n f v th k g s sh l r d t ch j b p** +
  short vowels **i e u a o** (keyword anchors include Itchy/Eddy/Upper/Apple/Olive
  — the same vowel anchors Barton uses).

## How this maps to games + family info (for the build, later)
The four bricks map cleanly onto game types (and reuse our existing oral /
phonemic-awareness engine + the per-item mastery model):
- **HEAR → "Same or Different?" / "Which did you hear?"** — discrimination on a
  confusable pair (m/n, f/v, p/b…), audio-first, no letters.
- **SEQUENCE → "Build the Sound Order"** — play a sequence (M‑N‑M), child rebuilds
  it with colored tiles / sound-pictures; length grows with mastery.
- **RETAIN → memory variants** — delay before rebuild; longer sequences.
- **UNDERSTAND → manipulation** — add/remove/change one sound in a sequence.
- Per-lesson mastery (which pairs a child confuses) feeds the same family
  dashboard "areas to improve" + a tutor view — the data parents pay to see.
- Family-facing **information**: what FIS is, why auditory skills matter before
  letters, the 4 Bricks, and their child's progress per brick / per sound pair.
